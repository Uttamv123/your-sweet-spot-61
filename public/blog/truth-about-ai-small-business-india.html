Small Businesses in India:
What Works and What Doesn't

Cost vs ROI: The Honest Numbers

The biggest mistake small businesses make is treating AI as a one-time purchase. It's not. There's setup cost, subscription cost, training cost, and the hidden cost of your time. Here's what a realistic first-year picture looks like:

Crunching the real numbers before committing to an AI tool subscription.

AI Tool / Use Case Monthly Cost (₹) Typical ROI Break-even
WhatsApp chatbot (WATI / AiSensy) 2,000 – 5,000 High (3–5×) 1–2 months
AI content writing (ChatGPT Plus) ~1,700 Medium–High 1 month
Inventory forecasting (Zoho / Odoo) 3,000 – 8,000 Medium (2–3×) 3–6 months
Custom ML model (bespoke build) 50,000 – 2,00,000 Variable / risky 12–24 months
AI HR / recruitment tools 5,000 – 15,000 Low for <20 employees Often never
AI accounting (ClearTax / Vyapar AI) 1,500 – 4,000 High (4–6×) 1–2 months

The pattern is clear: off-the-shelf AI tools with a defined task deliver fast ROI. Custom-built AI with vague goals burns money. If a vendor can't tell you exactly what metric improves and by how much, walk away.

Rule of thumb: if the AI tool doesn't pay for itself within 90 days, it's solving the wrong problem.

Common Mistakes That Cost Businesses Dearly

These aren't hypothetical. Each one below is a pattern seen in real Indian SMBs that spent money on AI and ended up worse off than before.

When the tool is more complex than the problem it was meant to solve.

Mistake 1

Buying AI before defining the problem. "We need AI" is not a business requirement. Start with the pain point — slow customer responses, high cart abandonment, inaccurate stock levels — then find a tool that fixes that specific thing.

Mistake 2

Paying for a custom build when a SaaS exists. Dozens of Indian startups are selling ₹10 lakh custom AI builds for problems that a ₹3,000/month tool already solves. Do your research before commissioning anything custom.

Mistake 3

Ignoring language and context. A chatbot trained on English data will frustrate your Hindi or Marathi-speaking customers. Always validate that the tool works in your customers' language — not just English demos.

Mistake 4

No owner for the AI tool internally. AI tools don't run themselves. If nobody on your team is responsible for monitoring, updating, and improving it, it will degrade and become a liability. Assign ownership before you buy.

Mistake 5

Treating AI as a replacement for bad processes. If your existing process is broken, AI will execute the broken process faster. Fix the workflow first, then automate it.

Mistake 6

Chasing the latest trend instead of proven tools. Every month there's a new AI tool being hyped. Stick to tools with a track record, local support, and case studies from businesses similar in size and industry to yours.

The Bottom Line

AI is not magic, but it's also not overhyped for the right use cases. For small businesses in India, the wins are real — faster customer response, less manual bookkeeping, better inventory decisions, cheaper marketing. The losses are also real — wasted subscriptions, custom projects that never shipped, and tools nobody uses.

The difference between the two groups isn't budget or technical skill. It's clarity of purpose. Know exactly what problem you're solving. Pick the simplest tool that solves it. Measure the result in 90 days. That's the entire playbook.
